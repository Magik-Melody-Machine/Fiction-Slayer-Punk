While I love the thought behind this, and you are absolutely free to use whichever words you like, I do want to touch on your "why" for a moment. Time and time again people have made terms to try and separate themselves from the clinical view of plurality. They always claim it's a form of avoiding gatekeeping, but, in reality all it does is separate plural communities and cause an even greater divide. Speaking as a traumaendo system myself, who likely has OSDD, I find it a bit upsetting that there's such a strong push to divide both communities even though we all experience pretty much the same thing. That division is actually why it took me so long to even accept my own plurality, as I felt like I was being pitted against both communities.

Also, every time a term has been created to divide these communities even further, if this were to catch on by any means, then it would only reach the disordered community anyway. Thats what happened to pretty much every term ever made to separate disordered plurality from non disordered plurality.

Again, I really do understand where you are coming from and I love the motive behind it but, I can't exactly say that it's kind to both sides either. Making a division all for the sake of disliking division, is itself division. It's drawing an imaginary line where a line really just doesn't need to be. The issue with gatekeeping isn't resolved by drawing a new line In the sand, it's by finding unity and commonalities. And the best way to do that is by sharing spaces, and sharing terminology. You won't appease gatekeepers just because you use different terms. Gatekeepers don't dislike you for using clinical terms, they dislike you because they are misguided. It's a reflection of themselves, not you. I should know, I used to be one of them. The reason why I stopped being a sysmed in the first place was because of endos using plural terms in non clinical ways. I was able to see the similarities between communities which is what caused me to eventually change my mind, and start accepting all other forms of plurality.

I can tell you put a lot of creativity in this and I sincerely do not mean to bash you or belittle your reasoning in any way. I just think it's worth thinking about the other side of the equation. That's all
